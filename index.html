Dr. rer. nat. Pawan Goyal

He is a postdoctoral researcher at Max Planck Institute (MPI-DCTS), Magdeburg, Germany, and currently working with Prof. Dr. Peter Benner. In 2018, he received his Ph.D. degree in the field of applied mathematics in his group as well.

He was awarded the prize for the best Ph.D. thesis within the Faculty of Mathematics by the Otto von Guericke University Magdeburg, Germany, and he was also awarded Dr. Klaus Körper prize by GAMM for his excellent Ph.D. thesis in the field of Applied Mathematics and Mechanics.

Research Interests

  • Data-driven learning of dynamical process
  • Dimensionality reduction and model-order reduction
  • Image reconstruction and applications in bio-materials
  • Denoising and matrix/tensor completion problems

    Recent News

    • 31/10/2019: Towards (structured) system identification and arising intersest in port-Hamiltonian systems due to its vast applications in physics and engineering, in our new preprint, we have proposed an algorithm to identify such systems from an exprimental data (frequency response). The title of the paper is "Identification of Port-Hamiltonian Systems from Frequency Response Data" and is now avaiable on arXiv.

    • 30/10/2019: We have submitted our new work, where we have proposed a simple yet very powerful algorithm, so-called "DROP" to determine low-dimensional models for structured parametric systems. The title of the paper is "Identification of Dominant Subspaces for Linear Structured Parametric Systems and Model Reduction" and is now avaiable on arXiv.
